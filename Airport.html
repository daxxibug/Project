Rome Fiumicino Airport (FCO)

Official Name: Leonardo da Vinci International Airport

This is the busiest and largest airport in Italy. Located about 32 km from Rome, it serves as the primary hub for ITA Airways and the main entry point for international and intercontinental travelers. In 2025, it famously broke the threshold of 51 million annual passengers, solidifying its place as one of Europe's top aviation hubs.

Milan Malpensa Airport (MXP)

Official Name: Aeroporto Internazionale di Milano-Malpensa "Silvio Berlusconi"

The largest international airport in Northern Italy, Malpensa handles the majority of the country's air cargo and long-haul flights outside of Rome. It is situated about 50 km from Milan and is a critical gateway for those heading to the Italian Alps, the Lakes region, or the fashion capital itself.

Milan Bergamo Airport (BGY)

Official Name: Il Caravaggio International Airport

Though located in the city of Bergamo, it is considered Milan's third airport. It is famous for being a major low-cost hub (primarily for Ryanair). It is a popular choice for budget-conscious travelers and those heading to ski resorts in the North.
