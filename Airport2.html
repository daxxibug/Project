Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport (BLQ)

Official Name: Aeroporto Guglielmo Marconi di Bologna

Located in the heart of the "Food Valley", this airport is a strategic hub for the Emilia-Romagna region. It is highly efficient for travelers visiting nearby cities like Florence or Modena and is known for its Marconi Express monorail that connects the airport to the central train station in just 7 minutes.

Milan Linate Airport (LIN)

Official Name: Enrico Forlanini Airport"

Unline Malpensa, Linate is the "city airport" of Milan, located just 7km from the city center. It primarily handles domestic and short-haul European flights. It is highly favored by business travelers due to its proximity to Milan's financial district.

Palermo Airport (PMO)

Official Name: Falcone-Borsellino Airport

Sicily's second-busiest airport, named in memory of the two anti-mafia judges Giovanni Falcone and Paolo Borsellino. It is situated along the coast, offering passengers a dramatic view of the sea upon landing. It serves as the main gateway to western Sicily.

Pisa International Airport (PSA)

Official Name: Galileo Galilei Airport"

This is the main airport for the Tuscany region. While Florence has its own airport, Pisa often handles more international traffic and budget airlines. It is located remarkably close to the city center-you can actually see the Leaning Tower from the vicinity of the airport.
