Hotel de la Ville

A vibrant 18th-century palazzo perched at the top of the Spanish Steps. balancing Roman opulence with a rigorous sustainability program.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Lazio (Rome), Italy
  • Certification: Gold EarthCheck Certified
  • Primary Sustainability Focus: Elimination of single-use plastics and intensive local community engagement.
  • Known for: Its panoramic rooftop terrace and historic 18th-century architecture.

Lefay REsort & Spa Lago di Garda

A pioneer in sustainable luxury, this resort was designed to integrate into the landscape and operate with a completely carbon-neutral footprint.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Lombardy (Lake Garda), Italy
  • Certification: Green Clobe and ISO 14001
  • Primary Sustainability Focus: Use of renewable energy (biomass and solar) and total carbon offset.
  • Known for: Its award-winning "Scientify Clinic" and breathtaking views of Lake Garda.

Hotel Danieli

Located just steps from St. Mark's Square, this legendary hotel occupies three Venetian palaces and maintains strict environmental standards despite its history age.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Veneto (Venice), Italy
  • Certification: Green key
  • Primary Sustainability Focus: Water conservation and waste management within the delicate Venetian ecosystem.
  • Known for: Its 14th-centry Gothic architecture and its history as a residence for royalty.

Borgo Egnazia

A meticulous recreation of a traditional Apulian village, this resort champions the "Km 0" philosophy, using local materials and resources.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Puglia, Italy
  • Certificate: ISO 14001
  • Primary Sustainable Focus: Hyper-local food sourcing (Km 0) and the preservation of indigenous flora.
  • Known for: Its unique design using white Tufo stone and its Michelin-starred restaurant.
  • Annual visitors: ≈7 million
