Villa d'Este

A former royal residence on Lake Como that combines Renaissance grandeur with modern "Green Globe" Sustainable management.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Lombardy (Lake Como), Italy
  • Certification: Green Globe
  • Primary Sustainability Focus: Conservation of its 25-acre part and efficient thermal energy usage.
  • Known for: Its iconic floating pool and status as one of the world's most legendary hotels.

NH Collection Firenze Porta Rossa

Italy's oldest hotel (dating back to the 12th century) proves that ancient heritage and modern sustainability can coexist.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Tuscany (Florence), Italy
  • Certification: Green Key/BREEAM
  • Primary Sustainability Focus: Energy-efficient lighting and high-tech climate control within a historic shell.
  • Known for: Its 13th-century Monalda Tower and authentic frescoes.

Hotel Bristol Palace

An Art Nouveau masterpiece in Genoa, located relatively near Sanremo, that is a leader in urban energy efficiency.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Liguria (Genoa), Italy
  • Certification: Green Key
  • Primary Sustainability Focus: Sustainable procurement and rigorous staff training on environmental impact.
  • Known for: Its stunning elliptical staircase and classic Italian elegance.

Four Seasons Hotel Firenze

Set within the largest private garden in Florence, this hotel focuses on urban biodiversity and the restoration of historical greenery.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Tuscany (Florence), Italy
  • Certification: Green Key
  • Primary Sustainability Focus: Biodiversity preservation in the Gherardesca Garden and plastic-free guest experiences.
  • Known for: Its museum-quality art and the 11-acre botanical garden on the grounds.
