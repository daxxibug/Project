Archaeological Diving (Baia Underwater Park)

For adventure tourists, Italy offers a "Sunken Pompeii" near Naples.The Parco Sommerso di Baia allows tourists to snorkel or scuba dive over ancient Roman villas, mosaics, and statues that are now underwater due to volcanic activity. It is one of the world's most unique archaeological recreational sites.

Coastal Hiking (Sentiero degli Dei)

The "Path of the Gods" is the most famous coastal trail on the Amalfi Coast.This cliffside trek connects small villages like Positano and Agerola. It is a "bucket-list" item for international tourists because it offers a 360° view of the Mediterranean from heights that cars cannot reach.

Historical Cycling Tours (The Appian Way)

Cycling tourism (Cicloturismo) has exploded in Italy, supported by new "green-way" infrastructures.Tourists rent e-bikes to ride the Via Appia Antica in Rome—the world’s oldest highway. It allows travelers to see catacombs and ancient ruins at a slow, eco-friendly pace without the city’s heavy traffic.
