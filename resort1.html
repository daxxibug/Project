Taormina

Taormina is a picturesque hilltop town on Sicily’s eastern coast, overlooking the Ionian Sea and framed by Mount Etna. Known since antiquity for its Greek theater and stunning coastal vistas, it has long attracted artists, writers, and travelers for its blend of classical ruins, medieval streets, and Mediterranean charm.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Sicily, Italy
  • Founded: circa 4th century BCE (ancient Tauromenion)
  • Population: about 10,000 residents
  • Elevation: ~200 m (660 ft) above sea level
  • Famous for: Teatro Greco, Isola Bella, and panoramic sea-volcano views

History and setting

TFounded by Greek colonists from Naxos, Sicily around 396 BCE, Taormina (then Tauromenion) later flourished under Roman, Byzantine, and Norman rule. Its position on Monte Tauro offered both strategic defense and dramatic scenery. From the 18th century onward, it became a favorite stop on the Grand Tour and a retreat for European artists such as Johann Wolfgang von Goethe and Truman Capote.

Rimini

Rimini is a coastal city in Italy’s Emilia-Romagna region, located along the Adriatic Sea. Founded as the Roman colony Ariminum in 268 BC, it has evolved into one of Europe’s best-known seaside resorts. Rimini is celebrated for its 15 kilometers of sandy beaches, vibrant nightlife, and deep historical and cultural heritage linked to both ancient Rome and the Renaissance.

Key Facts:

  • Region: Emilia-Romagna, Italy
  • Founded: 268 BC (as Ariminum)
  • Population: ~150,000 (2023)
  • Coastline: 15 km of beaches on the Adriatic Sea
  • Notable figure: Filmmaker Federico Fellini

History and heritage

Rimini’s Roman roots remain visible in monuments such as the Arch of Augustus (27 BC) and the Tiberius Bridge (completed 21 AD). The Domus del Chirurgo, or Surgeon’s House, preserves one of the world’s richest collections of Roman surgical tools. During the Renaissance, the city flourished under the Malatesta family; their legacy endures in Tempio Malatestiano, designed by Leon Battista Alberti, and the fortified Castel Sismondo. In modern times, Rimini became Italy’s first organized seaside resort (1843), setting the model for European beach tourism.
