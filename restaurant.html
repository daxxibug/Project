Osteria Francescana (Modena)

Ranked multiple times as the best restaurant in the world, Chef Massimo Bottura’s three-star establishment is a temple of modern Italian gastronomy. The restaurant is famous for "re-inventing" traditional Emilian ingredients through the lens of contemporary art and childhood memories.

Five Famous Dishes:

  • Five Ages of Parmigiano Reggiano
  • The Crunchy Part of the Lasagna
  • Oops! I Dropped the Lemon Tart
  • An Eel Swimming Up the Po River
  • Beautiful, Psychedelic Spin-Painted Veal

La Pergola (Rome)

Located atop the Rome Cavalieri hotel, this is the only restaurant in Rome to hold three Michelin stars. Chef Heinz Beck focuses on light, Mediterranean-inspired flavors paired with an unparalleled panoramic view of St. Peter’s Basilica.

Five Famous Dishes:

  • Fagotelli "La Pergola"
  • King Prawns in Tempura
  • Black Cod with Celery Sauce
  • Amberjack Marinated in White Balsamic
  • Iced Sphere of Pomegranate

Da Vittorio (Brusaporto)

Run by the Cerea family, this restaurant is a bastion of Lombard excellence. It is particularly world-renowned for its seafood—a rarity for a restaurant located inland—and its incredibly warm, family-oriented hospitality.

Famous Dishes:

  • Paccheri alla Vittorio
  • Uovo all'Uovo (Egg to the Egg)
  • Pigeon with Lemon and Pistachio

Enoteca Pinchiorri (Florence)

Housed in a 16th-century palace, this Florentine icon is as much about the wine as it is about the food. Giorgio Pinchiorri’s wine cellar is one of the most valuable in the world, while the kitchen serves refined Tuscan-French fusion.

Famous Dishes:

  • Saffron Egg Bombolone
  • Tagliatelle with Rocket Butter
  • Vanilla Soufflé with Panforte Ice Cream
